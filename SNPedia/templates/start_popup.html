Before we begin

  • Do not consider it a trusted medical advice. Although it's taken a source (SNPedia) whose authors surely put lots of effort to make it correct, treat it as some information found on the Internet. It can be a good starting point for more research, not a definitive answer.
  • Not the whole picture. Many variants are not understood, many are not popular enough to draw statistical conclusions or even to enter a database.
    • Even if you find a gene variant that increases the probability of some illness by 20%, you may have some other gene decreasing it by 30% that this analysis missed.
    • Most traits are complex, with many genes interacting. Looking at variants in isolation (like this app does) often doesn't reveal the whole picture.
    • Some illnesses are actually many bad mutations that keep popping up and are relatively quickly removed from the gene pool, so they don't enter databases.
  • Not for the faint of heart. Most reports are usually about the increased probability of disease risk - probably that's where most of the research goes. Keep in mind that even a 3x increase in something extremely rare is still extremely rare.
  • Genes load the gun, but the environment pulls the trigger. Even if your genes increase the predisposition to something, in many cases lifestyle plays a bigger role.
